Selected Essays in Philosophy.

Essence of Humanity - Dated: 2024 - Page:

Abstract: The following essay investigates imagination as being central to the human essence, or what it is to be and feel like a human. First, imagination is formulated as prevalent only through desire. Then, desire and imagination are correlated such that the strength of imagination is proportional to the degree one desires. This correlation is proven to determine a classification system for how much imagination beings possess based on their basic desires and potential for complex desires. This then allows differentiation between “animals” and humans by postulating that only humans can follow higher-order desires, and thus be able to form strong, complex desires in stability. Therefore, the conclusion is reached that humans are fundamentally different from animals because of their ability to imagine to a “higher degree”.

Impact: As a speculative work, 'Essence of Humanity' is meant to introduce a new hypothesis into epistemology and the broader field of philosophy that allows for the definitive classification of humans in comparison to other living species, therefore answering the important question as to what exactly is a 'human'.

Philosophy for the Practical Man - Dated: 2024 - Page:

Abstract: A short essay exploring the academic subject of philosophy as more suitable for hobbyists (or "practical men"), as opposed to academic professionals.

Impact: The short essay is meant to inspire hobbyists and professionals to explore the subject in an artistic way that is meaningful, rather than in a way that simply glorifies it and its past practitioners.

Political Op-Eds and Essays

Democrats! See no Evil, Hear no Evil, Speak no Evil - Dated: 2025 - Page:

Abstract: On the urgent matter of getting young democrats to understand and interpret information as well as the perspectives of others properly. The main thesis is that the democratic party, no less any political party, will be able to survive in extremism, and in order to put their ambitions into effect, they must learn how to properly listen to the demands of the people and how to reconcile these demands with the party's historical desires.

Impact: Acknowledging the weakness in extremism and blindness to outside opinion is crucial for a successful political campaign. This op-ed hopes to inspire young democrats to change the course of the party's history through global consideration.

Democrats are Leaving their own Behind with Texas - Dated: 2025 - Page:

Abstract: Democrats didn't just leave the state of Texas, they also abandoned the Texan democrats by not standing up for them and ceded control to the republican party by running when it mattered most.

Impact: This op-ed is written with the hope that democrats will learn that compromise, however small it may be, is better than making "bold" moves that accomplish nothing but provoking false revelry.

Selected Projects.

Physlet Software - Dated: 2025-present - Page: https://github.com/rasimoon/Physlet

Abstract: A program intended to aid in physics education via allowing the proper visualization of graphs and training physicists in connecting physical phenomena with their graphical and mathematical representations.

Impact: Provides educators and curious students with the ability to participate in digital physics labs tailored towards their curriculum. Its being open source allows its reach to extend to all who are interested in learning physics.

The Bipartisan - Dated: 2025-present - Page: https://thebipartisan.substack.com/

Abstract: A weekly newsletter that I write featuring my analysis of the good and bad of novel republican thought. The point of the newsletter is to provide democrats with a republican perspective, as well as perspectives from the general public, so that new members of the democratic party can find some compromise between these different perspectives and their own to cultivate interest in the democratic party for the 2028 election year.

Impact: Provides democrats with the opinions needed to empower an age where they are observant to the needs of the public and, subsequently, the republican party.

Phi Lambda Phi - Dated: 2024-present - Page: Phi Lambda Phi

Abstract: A collection of guidelines for a student-led philosophy education initiative that aims for students and teachers to collaborate on ensuring philosophy education in schools through providing advice and notes on formal philosophy. The goal of the program is also to help students apply philosophy to their daily lives in the hopes that they are uplifted via critical thinking and problem solving, something that is best represented by them writing an essay on a topic of their choosing at the end of every year involved in the program.

Impact: Provides students in public schools with an education in philosophy and the ability to apply this education for their own enrichment, as well as the enrichment of their colleagues and the general community.
